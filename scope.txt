1. Introduction
Digital learning platforms are widely used in universities, but most focus on managing courses, distributing content, and recording grades. They function as administrative tools rather than intelligent learning systems and do not analyze how knowledge is structured or how students develop conceptual understanding. As a result, students often progress through courses without fully mastering prerequisite concepts. Teachers also face difficulty identifying which concepts students struggle with, as traditional systems provide only overall scores. AI tools may answer questions, but they are often disconnected from the curriculum and cannot provide structured guidance.
To address this gap, ConceptIntel is an AI-powered concept intelligence platform supporting both teachers and students. It organizes course content as interconnected concepts in a knowledge graph, provides adaptive learning paths for students using Agentic AI to observe, analyze, plan, and act on learning needs, and generates personalized learning materials such as flashcards, study guides, and quizzes using Generative AI. The system also checks assignments in and provides explainable feedback, helping students identify gaps while allowing teachers to review, edit, and approve all AI-generated outputs for transparency.

3. Problem Statement
Most digital learning platforms used in universities primarily focus on administrative tasks such as course management, content distribution, and grading. While these systems make it easier to deliver educational material, they provide very limited insight into how students actually understand and connect the underlying concepts of a subject. As a result, students often progress through courses without fully mastering prerequisite concepts, which leads to fragmented knowledge and cumulative learning gaps. Teachers also face difficulty identifying exactly which concepts students struggle with, since most platforms only provide overall grades rather than detailed concept-level analysis. Although modern AI tools can generate explanations, they are usually disconnected from the course curriculum and do not track student learning progress. Therefore, there is a need for an intelligent system that organizes educational content into interconnected concepts, tracks concept mastery, and provides adaptive guidance to improve both teaching and learning outcomes.
4. Problem Solution / Proposed Solution
ConceptIntel is an AI-powered concept intelligence platform that supports both students and teachers. It organizes course content into a knowledge graph, mapping concepts and their prerequisite relationships, allowing teachers to review, edit, and supervise curriculum structure. For students, the system leverages Agentic AI to provide adaptive learning paths, while Generative AI creates personalized learning materials based on concept mastery. It also checks assignments in soft form and provides explainable feedback, helping students understand exactly where they have gaps. Teachers can supervise all AI-generated content, approve outputs, and guide regeneration of materials when needed. By combining conceptlevel intelligence, adaptive learning, assignment feedback, and teacher oversight, The platform enhances student learning outcomes while enabling instructors to optimize instruction efficiently.

5. Objectives
The objectives of the proposed system include:
· Develop a web-based AI platform supporting both students and teachers. · Extract key concepts from course materials and organize them into a knowledge
graph. · Track concept-level mastery for students. · Generate adaptive learning materials including flashcards, study guides, quizzes, and
assignments in soft form with explainable feedback. · Provide teachers with actionable insights into learning gaps, bottlenecks, and student
progress. · Enable teacher supervision and approval of all AI-generated content, assignments,
and adaptive recommendations. · Reinforce weak concepts using personalized guidance and gamified exercises. · Support continuous content improvement through feedback-driven regeneration.
6. Scope
The scope of ConceptIntel is to develop a prototype web-based learning system supporting both students and teachers across three selected subjects: Applied Physics, Digital Logic Design, and Calculus & Analytical Geometry. Applied Physics is considered a prerequisite for Digital Logic Design to demonstrate prerequisite-based concept relationships.
Teachers can upload course materials such as slides, PDFs, textbooks, CLOs, PLOs, reference books, and HEC course outlines. If complete course content is not available, the system can generate an initial concept structure using outlines and reference materials. The system extracts concepts from uploaded materials and organizes them into a knowledge graph. Teachers can review, edit, and supervise the knowledge graph to monitor curriculum structure and student learning gaps.
Students follow adaptive learning paths using personalized quizzes, study guides, flashcards, and assignments in soft form (Word/PDF). Assignments include explainable feedback to highlight learning gaps, while teachers can review and validate AI-generated outputs.

The knowledge graph will be shared among teachers teaching the same course to maintain curriculum alignment. The system will also store knowledge graphs and learning resources so that future instructors can reuse existing course knowledge, preserving organizational knowledge. The system tracks progress at the concept level, reinforces weak concepts, and provides analytics and actionable insights for instructors. The project focuses on prototype implementation rather than full-scale deployment.
7. Modules
The proposed system will be implemented through a set of integrated modules that support course creation, concept intelligence, adaptive learning, and intelligent analytics.
7.1. User Authentication Module
Functions: · Sign up, login, and role-based access for students, teachers, and admins. · Manage user profiles and permissions. · Ensure secure authentication and session handling.
Input: User email, password, role Output: Authenticated session and role-based access
7.2. Course Management Module
Functions: · Create, edit, or delete courses with metadata (name, subject, semester, prerequisites). · Schedule courses by defining start/end dates for lessons, quizzes, and assignments. · Change course status (Draft / Open / Closed).
Input: Course name, subject, semester, prerequisites Output: Course created with unique course ID

7.5. Knowledge Graph Module
Functions: · Build and visualize knowledge graph with concept dependencies. · Allow teachers to edit concept relationships. · Highlight prerequisite concepts for adaptive learning paths. · Shared knowledge graph for teachers teaching the same course.

System Workflow:
The system operates through the following workflow: 1. User logs in to the system through the authentication module. 2. Teacher creates a course and defines prerequisites. 3. Students enroll in the course using an enrollment code. 4. Teacher uploads course content or outlines. 5. System extracts concepts and builds the knowledge graph. 6. System generates quizzes, flashcards, study material, and assignments. 7. Teacher reviews and approves AI-generated content. 8. Students attempt quizzes and submit assignments. 9. System evaluates assignments and identifies concept-level weaknesses. 10. Adaptive engine updates personalized learning paths. 11. Analytics dashboard shows student progress and concept mastery. 12. System sends notifications and updates to users.

8. System Limitations / Constraints
1. AI-based concept extraction may not always identify concepts perfectly and may require teacher adjustments
2. The system focuses on prototype functionality rather than full-scale deployment 3. Large-scale real-time adaptation may require more computational resources 4. The system relies on the quality of uploaded educational materials
9. Data Gathering Approach
Data for the system will primarily consist of educational materials such as lecture slides, textbooks, and course outlines. These materials will be processed using AI techniques to extract conceptual information. Additionally, user interaction data such as quiz responses and learning progress will be collected to analyze conceptual understanding. Guidance from academic supervisors and analysis of existing digital learning systems will also contribute to the system design.
